MORE INFO ABOUT MONUMENTS

Stonehenge

Stonehenge is a prehistoric monument located in Wiltshire, England, consisting of a ring of standing stones set within earthworks. Believed to have been constructed between 3000 and 2000 BC, its purpose remains a mystery, though it is thought to have had religious or astronomical significance. The site is famous for its alignment with the sun during solstices. Stonehenge is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most recognized and enigmatic landmarks in the world.

Red Square

Red Square is a historic city square located in the heart of Moscow, Russia. It is bordered by iconic landmarks such as the Kremlin, St. Basil's Cathedral, and the State Historical Museum. Historically, it has been the site of many significant events, including military parades and political demonstrations. Red Square is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a symbol of Russian history and culture, attracting millions of visitors each year.

Mount Rushmore

Mount Rushmore is a national memorial located in the Black Hills of South Dakota, USA. It features the 60-foot-high faces of four U.S. presidents—George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Theodore Roosevelt, and Abraham Lincoln—carved into granite. The monument was completed in 1941 and was designed by sculptor Gutzon Borglum. It symbolizes the nation's history and ideals and remains one of the most visited and iconic landmarks in the United States.

Niagara Falls

Niagara Falls is a world-renowned natural wonder located on the border between the United States and Canada. It consists of three waterfalls—the American Falls, Bridal Veil Falls, and Horseshoe Falls—spanning over 3,000 feet. The falls are known for their breathtaking beauty and immense power, with millions of gallons of water cascading every minute. Niagara Falls is a popular tourist destination and a symbol of nature's grandeur.

Edinburgh Castle

Edinburgh Castle is a historic fortress located on Castle Rock, a volcanic rock formation in the heart of Edinburgh, Scotland. The castle has been a royal residence and military stronghold for over 1,000 years. It is home to significant artifacts, including the Crown Jewels of Scotland and the Stone of Destiny. With stunning views of the city, Edinburgh Castle is one of Scotland's most popular tourist attractions and a symbol of its rich history.
