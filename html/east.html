MORE INFO ABOUT MONUMENTS

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications built primarily to protect against invasions from northern tribes. Stretching over 13,000 miles, it was constructed over several dynasties, starting as early as the 7th century BC. Today, it stands as one of the most iconic symbols of China’s historical strength and engineering.

Pyramids of Giza

The Pyramids of Giza, located near Cairo, Egypt, are among the most famous ancient structures in the world. Built around 4,500 years ago during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom, they were constructed as tombs for the Pharaohs. The Great Pyramid, built for Pharaoh Khufu, is the largest and was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. These monuments showcase the incredible architectural and engineering skills of ancient Egypt.

Petra

Petra, located in modern-day Jordan, is an ancient city famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system. Established as early as the 6th century BC by the Nabataeans, it was a thriving trade center. The city is renowned for its stunning structures, such as Al-Khazneh (the Treasury), carved directly into rose-colored sandstone cliffs. Petra became a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1985 and is often referred to as the "Rose City" due to the color of the stone.

Babylon

Babylon was an ancient city located in present-day Iraq, known for its impressive architecture and cultural achievements. It was the capital of the Babylonian Empire, especially during the reign of King Nebuchadnezzar II in the 6th century BC. Famous landmarks include the Ishtar Gate and the Hanging Gardens of Babylon, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Babylon played a significant role in the development of law, science, and architecture in the ancient world.

Dome of the Rock

The Dome of the Rock is an iconic Islamic shrine located in Jerusalem, on the Temple Mount. Completed in 691 CE, it is one of the oldest and most beautiful examples of Islamic architecture. The structure features a golden dome and is built over the rock where, according to tradition, the Prophet Muhammad ascended to heaven during the Night Journey. The site is also sacred to Jews and Christians, making it a significant religious landmark for multiple faiths.

Download History
Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat, located in Cambodia, is the largest religious monument in the world. Originally built in the early 12th century as a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Vishnu, it later became a Buddhist temple. Known for its stunning architecture and intricate bas-reliefs, Angkor Wat is a symbol of Cambodia's cultural heritage and is considered a masterpiece of classical Khmer architecture. The site is a UNESCO World Heritage site and attracts millions of visitors annually.
