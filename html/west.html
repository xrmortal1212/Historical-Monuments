MORE INFO ABOUT MONUMENTS

Big Ben

Big Ben is the nickname for the Great Bell of the clock at the north end of the Palace of Westminster in London, and often extended to refer to both the clock and the clock tower. The tower was completed in 1859 and is one of the most prominent symbols of the United Kingdom.

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer is an iconic statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Completed in 1931, it stands 98 feet tall and is located at the peak of the 2,329-foot Corcovado mountain in the Tijuca Forest National Park, overlooking the city. The statue is a symbol of Christianity and a cultural icon of both Rio de Janeiro and Brazil.

Colosseum

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an ancient amphitheater in Rome, Italy. Built between AD 70 and 80, it is the largest amphitheater ever built and could hold up to 80,000 spectators. The Colosseum was used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and other events. It remains one of the most iconic symbols of ancient Rome and is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It was named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower. Constructed from 1887 to 1889 as the entrance to the 1889 World's Fair, it is one of the most recognizable structures in the world and a global cultural icon of France.

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a colossal neoclassical sculpture on Liberty Island in New York Harbor. Designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and built by Gustave Eiffel, the statue was a gift from the people of France to the United States. It was dedicated on October 28, 1886, and has since become an enduring symbol of freedom and democracy.
